Dahlias are tuberous, herbaceous perennials native to Mexico and Central America. They are members of the Asteraceae family, which also includes sunflowers, daisies, and chrysanthemums. Dahlias come in a wide variety of colors, shapes, and sizes. Some dahlias have single flowers, while others have double or even triple flowers. The flowers can be as small as a few inches in diameter or as large as 12 inches across. Dahlias can grow to be as tall as 6 feet, but there are also dwarf varieties that only grow to be a few inches tall.

Dahlias are popular cut flowers and are often used in arrangements and bouquets. They can also be used to make dried flowers. Dahlias are a beautiful addition to any garden and can be enjoyed for many years.

DAISY

Daisy flowers are charming and delicate blooms that have captivated people for centuries. These lovely flowers are native to Europe but can now be found in various parts of the world due to their popularity.

Daisies are known for their distinctive appearance. They typically have a yellow center, called the disc floret, which is surrounded by a ring of white or colorful petals, known as ray florets. However, some daisies can also be found in shades of pink or purple, adding to their diversity and appeal.

Daisies have practical uses. In traditional medicine, daisy extracts have been used for their anti-inflammatory and soothing properties. They have been used to alleviate skin irritations, digestive issues, and even as a mild sedative

CALLA LILY

Calla lilies are elegant and distinctive flowers that have gained popularity for their unique beauty. Native to southern Africa, specifically regions like South Africa, Lesotho, and Swaziland, these graceful blooms have become popular ornamental plants worldwide.

One of the defining features of calla lilies is their distinctive shape. The flower consists of a long, smooth, trumpet-shaped spathe that gracefully curves and tapers at the end. This spathe is usually white or cream-colored, but there are also varieties available in shades of pink, yellow, and even deep purple. Emerging from the spathe is a cylindrical structure known as the spadix, which bears tiny, densely-packed flowers

CAMELLIA

Camellias are enchanting flowering plants that have captivated gardeners and flower enthusiasts for centuries. Belonging to the genus Camellia, these evergreen shrubs are native to East Asia, particularly China and Japan. They are known for their exquisite blossoms, glossy foliage, and significant cultural and symbolic importance.

One of the most notable features of camellias is their stunning flowers. They come in a wide range of colors, including shades of white, pink, red, and even variegated patterns. The flowers have a distinct structure, with layers of petals forming a harmonious composition. Some camellia species bloom in the fall or winter, providing a burst of color and beauty during the colder months when other flowers may be scarce.

HYDRANGEA

Hydrangeas are captivating flowering shrubs that have become beloved for their abundant and stunning blooms. Native to Asia and the Americas, these versatile plants belong to the Hydrangeaceae family and encompass several species and cultivars

One of the most striking features of hydrangeas is their large, showy flower clusters, known as inflorescences. These inflorescences come in a variety of shapes, including mophead, lacecap, and panicle. Additionally, hydrangea blooms exhibit a wide range of colors, such as pink, blue, white, purple, and even green, depending on the acidity of the soil they grow in.
