Dahlia

Dahlias are tuberous, herbaceous perennials native to Mexico and Central America. They are members of the Asteraceae family, which also includes sunflowers, daisies, and chrysanthemums. Dahlias come in a wide variety of colors, shapes, and sizes. Some dahlias have single flowers, while others have double or even triple flowers. The flowers can be as small as a few inches in diameter or as large as 12 inches across. Dahlias can grow to be as tall as 6 feet, but there are also dwarf varieties that only grow to be a few inches tall.

Dahlias are popular cut flowers and are often used in arrangements and bouquets. They can also be used to make dried flowers. Dahlias are a beautiful addition to any garden and can be enjoyed for many years.

DAISY

Daisy flowers are charming and delicate blooms that have captivated people for centuries. These lovely flowers are native to Europe but can now be found in various parts of the world due to their popularity.

Daisies are known for their distinctive appearance. They typically have a yellow center, called the disc floret, which is surrounded by a ring of white or colorful petals, known as ray florets. However, some daisies can also be found in shades of pink or purple, adding to their diversity and appeal.

Daisies have practical uses. In traditional medicine, daisy extracts have been used for their anti-inflammatory and soothing properties. They have been used to alleviate skin irritations, digestive issues, and even as a mild sedative

CALLA LILY

Calla lilies are elegant and distinctive flowers that have gained popularity for their unique beauty. Native to southern Africa, specifically regions like South Africa, Lesotho, and Swaziland, these graceful blooms have become popular ornamental plants worldwide.

One of the defining features of calla lilies is their distinctive shape. The flower consists of a long, smooth, trumpet-shaped spathe that gracefully curves and tapers at the end. This spathe is usually white or cream-colored, but there are also varieties available in shades of pink, yellow, and even deep purple. Emerging from the spathe is a cylindrical structure known as the spadix, which bears tiny, densely-packed flowers

CAMELLIA

Camellias are enchanting flowering plants that have captivated gardeners and flower enthusiasts for centuries. Belonging to the genus Camellia, these evergreen shrubs are native to East Asia, particularly China and Japan. They are known for their exquisite blossoms, glossy foliage, and significant cultural and symbolic importance.

One of the most notable features of camellias is their stunning flowers. They come in a wide range of colors, including shades of white, pink, red, and even variegated patterns. The flowers have a distinct structure, with layers of petals forming a harmonious composition. Some camellia species bloom in the fall or winter, providing a burst of color and beauty during the colder months when other flowers may be scarce.

HYDRANGEA

Hydrangeas are captivating flowering shrubs that have become beloved for their abundant and stunning blooms. Native to Asia and the Americas, these versatile plants belong to the Hydrangeaceae family and encompass several species and cultivars

One of the most striking features of hydrangeas is their large, showy flower clusters, known as inflorescences. These inflorescences come in a variety of shapes, including mophead, lacecap, and panicle. Additionally, hydrangea blooms exhibit a wide range of colors, such as pink, blue, white, purple, and even green, depending on the acidity of the soil they grow in.

LILAC

Lilacs are enchanting flowering shrubs or small trees that have captured the hearts of many with their delicate beauty and captivating fragrance. Belonging to the genus Syringa, lilacs are native to Europe and Asia, but they are now cultivated and adored in gardens and landscapes worldwide.

One of the most distinguishing features of lilacs is their lovely blossoms. They typically appear in late spring or early summer, showcasing clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of purple, pink, white, and even yellow. Lilac flowers are composed of tiny, tubular florets that form a compact, dense inflorescence, creating a striking visual display.

VERBENA

Verbena, also known as vervain, is a versatile and charming flowering plant that has captured the attention of gardeners and herbalists for centuries. Belonging to the genus Verbena, this herbaceous perennial is native to Europe, Asia, and the Americas. Verbena is admired for its beautiful blooms, vibrant colors, and its rich historical and cultural significance.

One of the most notable features of verbena is its profusion of small, delicate flowers that form clusters or spikes. These flowers come in a variety of colors, including shades of purple, pink, blue, white, and red. Verbena blooms throughout the summer, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees with its nectar-rich flowers.

ROSE

Roses, the timeless and iconic flowers, have captivated people for centuries with their unparalleled beauty and enchanting fragrance. Belonging to the genus Rosa, roses are native to various regions around the world and have become one of the most beloved and popular flowers globally.

One of the most striking features of roses is their diverse range of colors, including shades of red, pink, white, yellow, orange, and even blue (through selective breeding). Each color carries its own symbolic meaning, with red roses often representing love and passion, pink roses signifying gratitude and admiration, white roses symbolizing purity and innocence, and yellow roses representing friendship and joy.

LARKSPUR

Larkspur, also known as Delphinium, is a stunning flowering plant that graces gardens with its tall spikes of vibrant and elegant blooms. Belonging to the Ranunculaceae family, larkspur is native to Europe and Asia and has become a popular choice for adding vertical interest and vivid colors to garden landscapes.

One of the defining features of larkspur is its tall, slender stalks that can reach impressive heights, often exceeding three feet. These sturdy stems bear numerous delicate flowers that grow in tightly packed clusters along the length of the spike. Larkspur flowers come in a wide range of shades, including blue, purple, pink, white, and even bi-colored varieties. Their distinctive shape and vibrant hues make them eye-catching additions to any garden or floral arrangement.

SUNFLOWER

Sunflowers, with their vibrant yellow petals and iconic large blooms, are widely recognized and cherished for their beauty and symbolism. Belonging to the genus Helianthus, sunflowers are native to North and Central America but have gained global popularity as one of the most beloved and recognizable flowers.

The most striking feature of sunflowers is their large, daisy-like flower heads that radiate with bright yellow petals. The flower heads can reach impressive sizes, often measuring up to 12 inches (30 cm) in diameter. At the center of the flower, a dense cluster of disk florets forms, surrounded by a ring of ray florets that resemble petals. Sunflowers can also come in other colors, including orange, red, and even bi-colored varieties.

COSMOS

Cosmos, with its delicate and daisy-like flowers, is a charming and graceful annual plant that adds a touch of elegance and beauty to gardens and landscapes. Belonging to the genus Cosmos, these plants are native to Mexico and are widely cultivated for their colorful blooms and feathery foliage.

Cosmos plants are versatile and can be used in different garden settings. They are commonly planted in borders, beds, and containers, adding height and a pop of color to the landscape. Cosmos flowers also make excellent cut flowers, lasting well in arrangements and bouquets..
